Spread continues as UK braces

The new coronavirus that causes the flu-like Covid-19 disease originated in Wuhan in China at the end of 2019, and has so far affected over 80,000 people worldwide, with over 2,700 deaths. The World Health Organisation have designated it a public health emergency of international concern

Reports that a woman in Japan has tested positive for the Covid-19 disease for a second time, after seemingly recovering, will alarm scientists and public health experts trying to control the spreading epidemic, and underlines how much we still do not know.

What you need to know

Should I be wearing a face mask?

Face masks offer some protection as they block liquid droplets. However, they do not block smaller aerosol particles that can pass through the material of the mask. The masks also leave the eyes exposed and there is evidence that some viruses can infect a person through the eyes.

What is Covid-19 - the illness that started in Wuhan?

It is caused by a member of the coronavirus family that has never been encountered before. Like other coronaviruses, it has come from animals. Many of those initially infected either worked or frequently shopped in the Huanan seafood wholesale market in the centre of the Chinese city.

In the UK Stay indoors and avoid contact with people. Call NHS 111 to inform of your recent travel.
